## 35kV Corridor Configuration for Harare
A practical Harare municipal reinforcement line in this case is the **35kV double-circuit steel tubular tower** arrangement using **approximately 191 poles** over **about 11km**. The geometry is compact: **22m tapered steel tubular poles**, **60m span**, **1.5m phase spacing**, **5.5m ground clearance**, and **0.8m insulator length**. For a dense urban feeder route, this is a distribution-level build rather than a 110kV or 220kV transmission corridor, and the stated configuration is sized for intra-city network reinforcement, road-reserve constraints, and substation-to-load-center connections.

The structural package is defined around **hot-dip galvanized Q345 steel**, with **anchor-bolt cage foundations** and a **30-year design life**. The mechanical design basis is **Wind Class 4 at 40m/s**, which is the key loading input for exposed municipal sections and open corridor segments. The conductor selection is **ACSR 70**, specified at **275kg/km** with **maximum tension 22kN**, which aligns with short urban spans and moderate conductor loading. The tower form is a **double circuit** arrangement, so the line can carry two circuits on the same structure envelope, reducing right-of-way consumption compared with separate single-circuit alignments.

The line geometry is tighter than a generic long-span transmission arrangement because the **60m span** is intended for urban crossings, road reserves, and sections where alignment flexibility is limited. In that context, the combination of **22m height** and **5.5m ground clearance** provides the vertical envelope needed for a medium-voltage feeder while keeping the structure compact enough for municipal corridors. The **1.5m phase spacing** and **0.8m insulator length** are part of the electrical clearance package and should be treated as fixed design inputs when checking structure-to-structure consistency across the route.

### Material and maintenance implications
Using **Q345 hot-dip galvanized steel** gives the structure a corrosion-resistant finish suitable for urban exposure and reduces routine maintenance compared with painted steel alternatives. The **anchor-bolt cage foundation** is the standard interface for transferring overturning and conductor loads into the substructure, and it simplifies repeatable installation across a long corridor with many identical pole locations. For procurement teams, the important point is that the specification is not a generic tower concept; it is a repeatable municipal distribution package with defined mechanical and electrical limits.
